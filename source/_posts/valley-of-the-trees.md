I wish I could explain the night sky near Lake Tahoe. It is not that I lack the words, nor that you lack imagination. In reality, I can’t see the night sky anymore. My memory knows what it is supposed to be, and my retinas know what they see. But my mind does this wonderful extrapolation between those two endpoints, creating something that is not true to my past, nor to my present.

Early Cataract is an exercise in defiance. Your body is able. Your mind is sharp. Each joint in you is well-oiled. And the not-so-old bones make you carry a confidence that anything can be solved. Larger font, unlit rooms, shades, hats, cold-water eye rubs, dark themes, disabling subtitles, giving up on sharpness.

The ego has it all figured out - the world is not well-defined any longer. But I am still able and sharp, to walk, to run, to drive.

The mind is a magical image processor. To be able to see what is unseen, to apply all different heuristics and unblur the road signs. No matter the name, the number, the color, the font, the distance.

I wonder what would happen if my cataract change based on the road taken. Different for different exits. Different for friends and foes. Different for left and right.

The cataract has it all figured out — the world cannot be defined any longer for the ego to thrive. The stars are special. They are out of this world.

As I walk in the valley of 300ft trees, contemplating how tall they really are, a spot creeps up on me that hides all the light from this world, and stars should now appear aplenty.

Can you count them … for me?
